Chester Bennington

Chester Bennington was an American singer, songwriter, actor and producer. He became famous as the lead vocalist of the rock band Linkin Park, one of the most popular and influential bands of the 21st century. He was also the vocalist of the bands Dead by Sunrise, Stone Temple Pilots, Grey Daze and Kings of Chaos.

Bennington was born in Phoenix, Arizona, on March 20, 1976. He had a difficult childhood, suffering from sexual abuse, bullying and drug addiction. He found refuge in music, especially in rock and metal. He started singing in local bands, such as Sean Dowdell and His Friends? and Grey Daze, but did not achieve the recognition he wanted.

In 1999, he auditioned for the band Xero, which later changed its name to Linkin Park. His powerful and emotional voice fit perfectly with the sound of the band, which blended rock, metal, rap and electronic music. The band's debut album, Hybrid Theory, was released in 2000 and became a worldwide success, selling over 30 million copies. The album was certified Diamond by the Recording Industry Association of America in 2005, making it the best-selling debut album of the decade, as well as one of the few albums ever to achieve that many sales. He continued to serve as the band's lead vocalist for their next six studio albums, from Meteora (2003) to One More Light (2017), with each charting within the top three spots of the Billboard 200.

Bennington also explored other musical projects, such as Dead by Sunrise, a alternative rock band that he formed with the members of Orgy and Julien-K. He also joined Stone Temple Pilots in 2013, replacing the late Scott Weiland as vocalist. He also collaborated with other artists, such as Jay-Z, Slash, Santana, Steve Aoki, Motley Crue and Chris Cornell.

Besides music, Bennington also ventured into film and television. He made appearances in movies such as Crank, Crank: High Voltage, Saw 3D and Artifact. He also participated in TV shows such as Saturday Night Live, Punk'd, The X Factor and Carpool Karaoke.

Bennington struggled with depression, anxiety and addiction to drugs and alcohol throughout his life, starting in his childhood. He was also affected by the death of his friend and idol, Chris Cornell, in May 2017. On July 20, 2017, on what would have been Cornell's 53rd birthday, Bennington committed suicide at his home in Palos Verdes Estates, California. He was 41 years old. He left behind his wife, Talinda, and six children.

Bennington was honored by his bandmates, family, friends and fans around the world. He was considered one of the best rock vocalists of all time, and his music inspired millions of people. He was also praised for his generosity, kindness and social activism. He supported various causes, such as suicide prevention, cancer awareness, animal protection and humanitarian aid.
